Composition

Spec

First title

The State has a duty to protect its citizens residing abroad as prescribed by law. Laws and treaties in effect at the time this Constitution enters into force shall remain in effect unless they are inconsistent with this Constitution. All citizens have the right to work. The State shall endeavor to promote employment and guarantee adequate wages for workers through social and economic means, and shall enforce a minimum wage system as prescribed by law.

Second title

All citizens shall have the right to freedom of residence inviolable. When conducting a search or seizure of a residence, a warrant issued by a judge upon the request of a prosecutor must be presented. The President shall serve as the Chairman of the State Council, and the Prime Minister shall serve as the Vice-Chairman. Members of the National Assembly shall not concurrently hold any other office prescribed by law. Members shall not be removed from office except by impeachment or a sentence of imprisonment or heavier punishment. Military courts may be established as special courts to exercise jurisdiction over military trials.

Third title

Military personnel cannot be appointed as State Council members unless they are discharged from active duty. Judges other than the Chief Justice and Justices of the Supreme Court are appointed by the Chief Justice with the consent of the Conference of Justices. The National Assembly has the right to consent to the conclusion and ratification of treaties relating to mutual assistance or security, treaties relating to important international organizations, treaties of friendship, commerce, and navigation, treaties relating to limitations on sovereignty, treaties of peace, treaties imposing significant financial burdens on the state or its people, or treaties relating to legislative matters.
